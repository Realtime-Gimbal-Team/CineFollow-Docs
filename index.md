# Realtime-CineFollow-Gimbal

## 1. Project Introduction

The **Realtime-CineFollow-Gimbal** is an intelligent dual-axis gimbal system designed for cinematography. In simple terms, it is a "thinking" camera mount. 

By receiving commands from external vision algorithms (such as face detection or object tracking), it utilizes high-performance brushless motors to adjust the camera's orientation in real-time, ensuring the subject remains centered in the frame. No matter how the subject moves, the gimbal provides cinematic, smooth tracking performance.

## 2. Disclaimer & Safety Warnings

> [!CAUTION]
> **CRITICAL SAFETY INFORMATION: READ BEFORE PROCEEDING**
> Failure to follow these instructions may result in permanent hardware damage or fire hazards.

### ⚡ Power Supply
The motor driver section of this project requires a **12V DC power supply**. 
* **DO NOT** connect a higher voltage. 
* Exceeding 12V will result in **permanent damage** to both the driver modules and the Raspberry Pi Pico controller.

### 🚫 No Hot-Swapping
**NEVER** plug or unplug motor cables (Pitch/Yaw interfaces) while the power is ON. 
* The transient back-EMF (Electromotive Force) generated by hot-swapping will **instantly destroy** the SimpleFOC driver modules. 
* Always ensure the power source is disconnected before making any wiring changes.

### 🔥 Stall Risk
If a motor is physically obstructed or becomes stuck during rotation (stalling):
* **Disconnect the power immediately.** * Prolonged stalling causes excessive current draw, which will lead to the MOS transistors overheating or **catching fire**.
